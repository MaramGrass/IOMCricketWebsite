A Growing Game

Women's Cricket on the Island

Women's and girls' cricket is one of the fastest-growing areas of the sport on the Isle of Man. With a thriving national team, dedicated club programmes and multiple pathways for young players, there has never been a better time to get involved.

Whether you want to pick up a bat for the first time or aim for the national team, the IOMCA has a programme and a pathway to help you achieve your goals.

How to Start

Your Cricket Journey

Soft Ball Cricket
The perfect entry point for beginners. No experience needed — just enthusiasm. Soft ball cricket is played at clubs across the Island in a friendly, social environment.
Find out more →
Hard Ball Cricket
For those ready to step up to traditional cricket. Clubs offer women's-specific training sessions and leagues providing competitive cricket for all standards.
Find a club →
National Team
The pinnacle of women's cricket on the Island. The IOMCA Women's National Team competes in ICC and Cricket Europe competitions against the best in Europe.
Meet the squad →
Academy & EPP
Talented young women players can access the IOMCA Academy and Emerging Players Programme to accelerate their development towards national selection.
Academy info →
Girls in Schools
The IOMCA delivers cricket into schools across the Island, providing girls with their first taste of cricket through fun, inclusive programmes.
Women's Training
Dedicated women's and girls' training sessions are available at clubs across the Island throughout the year, including indoor winter training.
